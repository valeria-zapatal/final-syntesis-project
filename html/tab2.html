Stars temperature

Color codes to consider a star cold or hot:

The surface color of the stars depends on their temperature and age, the youngest stars have a bluish color, and the oldest stars have a red color tone, this is due to if the star is young and has less mass, more energy and temperature it can reach.

However, the relation between their age and temperature is not universal, because if the star is bigger, their fuel will be consumed faster and will become in a red tonality in less time range. If the star has less mass, its “life” time will be more durable and it will become red in less time.



Electromagnetic spectrum:

The shades that a star acquires along their live can go from a red to a bluish tonality, being the last tonality the hottest. The electromagnetic spectrum explains this, talking about the ultraviolet and infrared light, being de last one the most intense, this implies that the blue color has stronger and energetic radiations, this is the reason why the stars with this color are the hottest in the classification of heat scale.


The woman that classified the stars:

Annie Jump Cannon, was the girl that discovered this technique of classifying stars according to their shape, size, color, and other characteristics, she was based in something called spectral classification. She explained that at the moment we look at the stars, there was always colored lines missing in the rainbow they emit, this is because there are different atoms and molecules with different shapes that can absorb different colors or light waves lengths.



The missing lines in the rainbow are called spectral and the pattern that they produce is different in each star, because every atom and particle absorb a different specifically amount of light, being a type of fingerprint, which depending of the density of the lines, this allow us to classify them with the letters: O, B, A, F, G, K and M. In the letter “O” we include the hottest, shiny and blue tonality stars, while in letter “M”, we find the “coldest” and red colored stars.




The melting process inside the sun that makes the electromagnetic radiation waves possible:

The process that the sun makes for its waves of electromagnetic energy to get to the earth is that two lightweight atoms of hydrogen and their isotopes merge to create a heavy nucleus, releasing a big amount of energy and helium, which get into the earth in an electromagnetic radiation way, short radiation waves mainly in ultraviolet, visible and infrared near with waves lengths between 200nm to 300nm. We have to consider that all that allows this process is the sun’s temperature (5,772K) since the nuclear fusion needs thermic energy (heat) to perform.
